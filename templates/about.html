      Rules:
    •  
       
        1. Eligibility Criteria Must be an Indian citizen. Must be at least 18 years old on the qualifying date (usually January 1st of the election year). Must be a resident of the constituency in which they wish to vote.
       
       
        2. Voter Registration Must be enrolled in the Electoral Roll of their constituency. To register: Submit Form 6 (available online and offline) to the Election Commission of India (ECI). Provide proof of age, identity, and residence.
       
       
        3. Possession of a Voter ID (EPIC) Having a Voter ID card (EPIC) is not mandatory for voting, but the name must be on the electoral roll. If no Voter ID, other accepted identification (e.g., Aadhaar card, passport, driving license) can be used.
       
       
        4. Absence of Disqualifications Cannot vote if declared of unsound mind by a competent court. Cannot vote if disqualified under laws related to electoral offenses.
       
       
       5. Special Cases NRIs (Non-Resident Indians): Eligible to register and vote but must be physically present in India on voting day. Service Personnel: Eligible to register as service voters and vote via postal ballots.
